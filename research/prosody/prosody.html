Prosodic resolution of syntactic/semantic ambiguity in Korean psych-predicate constructions

In a sentence like "민영이가 무서운 가 봐요," is 민영 experiencing the psychological state of fear or is someone else (not mentioned in the sentence) fearing 민영? My research shows that the two meanings of the ambiguous sentence are said with different prosodies. I initially explored only two different types of prosodic phrases (Acceptual Phrase and Intonational Phrase) in Ahn (2017) and Ahn (2020). But Ahn (2018) digged deep into the full range of Korean prosody and showed that there can be four differnt prosodic phrases, and the mapping between prosody and syntax is intervened through the information structure of the discourse.
